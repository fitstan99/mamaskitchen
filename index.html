⚠ Read this only if you can handle the math

Last year, you threw away
three weeks of your life.
And you paid extra for it.

Most people will never finish this page, because the number at the bottom is embarrassing. If you'd rather stay comfortable, click away now — Mom won't chase you. But if you're ready to see exactly what "I'll just cook it myself" has been quietly costing you, keep reading.

The math you've been avoiding

One meal is never "just cooking."

It's a chain of chores you quietly agreed to do, for free, forever. Add up one single home-cooked meal — protein and two sides — honestly:

Driving to the store and back~12 min
Wandering the aisles & waiting in line~10 min
Washing, peeling, chopping~14 min
Actually cooking it~22 min
Scrubbing the pans, loading the sink~12 min
Real cost of ONE plate≈ 70 minutes

An hour. For one plate. And then you do it again. And again. And again — like it's free. It isn't.

Now multiply it

This is where it starts to sting.

Say you eat ten of these a week. At an hour each, that's 10 hours a week. Over 43 hours a month. And across a year…

520+ hours

More than twenty full days. Three solid work-weeks — awake, on your feet, standing over a cutting board and a sink. Gone. And nobody refunded a single minute.

"You didn't lose minutes. You lost weeks — and you'll never get them back."

Think ten meals is a lot? Fine. Cut it in half. Five meals a week is still a full day, every single month, that vanished into errands and dishwater.

And that's only the time

Now let's talk about the money.

While the clock was robbing you, your wallet was bleeding right alongside it:

Food that rotted before you cooked it$1,500+/yr
"Too tired to cook" takeout$1,000–2,000/yr
Impulse junk grabbed while shopping hungry$800–1,500/yr
Quietly leaving your account, every year$3,000–5,000

Read that bottom number again. That money wasn't spent — it was wasted. It bought you nothing. No memory, no progress, no joy. It evaporated, in spoiled vegetables and soggy delivery bags.

Now picture what it could have been

Here's what that time and money actually bought you: nothing. Here's what it could have:

The vacation you keep "saving up for." It was sitting right there — in the food you let spoil and the dinners you handed to a delivery app. You didn't lack the money. You fed it to your trash can.

The body you swore you'd get to "when things calm down." Those were the gym hours. You spent them peeling potatoes.

The bedtime stories. The weekends. The evenings you were actually present for the people you love. Traded — quietly, night after night — for a sink full of dishes.

The side business. The raise. The skill. The thing you keep saying you'd build "if you only had time." You had the time. Over 500 hours of it. You washed it down the drain.

"You never chose any of this on purpose. But you chose it — one peeled potato at a time."
Let's be honest

You were meant for more than this.

You are not on this earth to be your own grocery clerk, line cook, and dishwasher. That is not your highest use. It never was. And somewhere underneath the busyness, you already know that — which is exactly why this page is uncomfortable to read.

Good. Stay uncomfortable for thirty more seconds.

The proof

The people you admire already solved this. They didn't out-work you on chores — they refused to do them.

Alex Hormozi — built a portfolio reportedly doing around $250 million a year — hammers one brutal rule: figure out what your hour is worth, then refuse to do anything beneath it. Delegate it, or delete it.
Warren Buffett keeps a famously near-empty calendar and treats his time as the one asset all his billions still can't buy back.
Steve Jobs wore the same black turtleneck. Mark Zuckerberg, the same grey shirt. Barack Obama wore only gray or blue suits — on purpose — so they'd never waste a drop of energy on a decision that didn't matter.
Elite athletes & top CEOs pay private chefs for one reason: so "what do I eat" is one more decision they never have to make again.
"The most successful people alive don't negotiate with their time. They guard it like the one thing they can't buy more of — because it is."

So ask the question they all asked: what is my highest use? Because I can promise you this — it is not standing at a sink at 9 p.m., scrubbing a pan.

Here's the part you can still change

You can't get last year back. You can stop the bleeding this Sunday.

You can't undo the weeks you've already lost. But you can refuse to lose one more. You take every hour you've been pouring into shopping, peeling, cooking, and scrubbing — and you hand it to Mom.

Fresh, high-protein meals, made for you, ready every Monday. No store. No prep. No dishes. One decision, made once — and your week is handled while you go live your actual life.

The only question left is the one that's been quietly costing you all along: how much is your time really worth?

Mom's already shopping. All you have to do is stop doing it yourself. 💛

Terms & Privacy

Mama's Kitchen, Provincetown, MA. Last updated 2025.

1. Orders, pre-payment & cancellations

All orders are pre-ordered and pre-paid in full before cooking. The weekly order deadline is Sunday at midnight EST. Meals are cooked fresh and made available for pickup or delivery on Monday, 3–6 PM; for larger orders we split the order for freshness — roughly half on Monday and half on Thursday, 3–6 PM. Because every meal is prepared fresh to your order, orders generally cannot be changed or refunded once cooking has begun. If something is wrong with your order, contact us right away and we'll make it right.

2. Freshness, storage & food safety

Meals are fresh, never frozen, and are best enjoyed within 3–4 days of pickup. Keep refrigerated. Reheat thoroughly before eating. Cooked weights are listed on labels; portion sizes are approximate.

3. Allergens

Our food is prepared in a kitchen that handles common allergens (including dairy, eggs, fish, shellfish, soy, wheat, and nuts). We cannot guarantee any item is free of a given allergen. Please list allergies and dietary needs at checkout, and contact us with any concerns before ordering.

4. SMS / text messaging consent

By checking the SMS box and providing your phone number, you consent to receive recurring transactional and order-related text messages from Mama's Kitchen at the number provided, including via automated technology. Consent is not a condition of any purchase beyond order updates. Message and data rates may apply. Reply STOP to unsubscribe or HELP for help.

5. Email & marketing consent

If you opt in, we may send you emails and occasional promotions or marketing. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in any email or by contacting us.

6. Data we collect & how we use it

To operate and improve the business we collect and store information you provide and that is generated by your orders, including: name, contact details, delivery address, age, your stated goal/reason for ordering, meal selections and history, order totals, and communication preferences. We use this data to fulfill orders, plan purchasing and production, understand demand (e.g. popular items and customer trends), and communicate with you. We do not sell your personal information. We share data only with service providers needed to run the business (e.g. payment processing via CardConnect/Fiserv, messaging/email delivery). Payment card details are handled directly by CardConnect — we never see or store your full card number.

7. Accounts

Ordering requires a free account so we can save your details, preferences, and order history. You are responsible for keeping your login secure and your information accurate.

8. Recurring weekly orders & automatic billing

If you opt into a recurring weekly order and authorize automatic billing, you agree that Mama's Kitchen may securely charge your saved payment method each week for that week's order total (which may vary if you change your plan), around the Sunday order deadline, until you pause or cancel. You can pause or cancel anytime from your account before the weekly deadline. Your card is stored securely as a token by CardConnect — we never see or store your full card number.

9. Pricing, taxes & fees

Prices are shown pre-tax. Massachusetts sales tax (6.25%) and a 3% card-processing fee are added at checkout. Prices and menu availability may change based on seasonal and local sourcing.

10. Contact

Questions about your order or these terms? Text us at +1 (213) 212-1290, DM @mamaskitchenptown, or use the chat feature. We're happy to help with any accommodations.
